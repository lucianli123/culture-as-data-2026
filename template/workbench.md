# The workbench (assessment portfolio)

The portfolio you present in Week 10, kept in your project repository from Week 4 on.
Four slots, plus a one-paragraph introduction written last.

## The slots

1. **The notebook.** Your analysis, runnable top to bottom by someone who is not you
   (Mullen's standard: "I must be able to run it"). Link the Colab; commit a copy.
2. **The Data Biography.** Written at commit (Week 4), revised if the corpus changed.
   Template: `data-biography.md` in this folder.
3. **One revised-after-feedback artifact.** A chart, a section, or a finding that
   changed because of peer critique or the one-on-ones — include the before, the after,
   and two sentences on what the feedback was.
4. **Check-out notes.** Your weekly check answers, kept as you go; they are the record
   of the competencies as they arrived.

## The workbench introduction (written in Week 10)

One paragraph: *here is what I can do now that I could not in Week 1*, mapped to the
eight competencies. Concrete beats comprehensive — three competencies with evidence
outweigh eight asserted.
