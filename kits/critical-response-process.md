# Critical Response Process, one page

The Week 9 critique protocol, run in trios (about ten minutes per maker). Adapted from
Liz Lerman's Critical Response Process. The order is the point: meaning before
questions, questions before opinions, and opinions only by permission.

## The four steps

**1. Statements of meaning (2 min).** Responders say what was meaningful, evocative, or
memorable in the draft. No "but," no advice hidden as praise. The maker listens.

**2. The maker asks (3 min).** The maker questions the responders about whatever they
want reactions to: "Did the second chart read as evidence or decoration?" Responders
answer the question asked — opinions only within its scope.

**3. Neutral questions (3 min).** Responders ask questions that carry no embedded
opinion. *"Why did you choose a log scale?"* is neutral. *"Don't you think a log scale
is misleading?"* is an opinion wearing a question mark; save it for step 4.

**4. Permissioned opinions (2 min).** "I have an opinion about the title — would you
like to hear it?" The maker may say no, and that answer costs nothing.

## Why this order

Drafts die in critique when opinions arrive first: the maker defends instead of
listening, and the responders perform taste instead of reading. Meaning first proves the
work was received; the maker's own questions aim the feedback where it is wanted; neutral
questions surface choices the maker didn't know they had made. By the time opinions
arrive, they land on a maker who has already been heard three times.

## For the facilitator

Keep time visibly. Interrupt disguised opinions in step 3, kindly and every time — the
room learns the distinction fast. If a trio finishes early, a second round of step 3 is
worth more than a longer step 4.
