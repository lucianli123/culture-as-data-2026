The Ten Weeks

Three acts: Tour (1–3), Project (4–7), Publish (8–10). Each week opens with a finished study you first admire, then take apart, and lands on something you build.

WeekTitleTool / methodThe promise
Week 1Your First InvestigationCulture as data itself (the ladder starts next week)By the break you'll have loaded a real dataset, asked it a question, and produced your first chart. A complete investigation, end to end, on day one.
Week 2Counting Is Already a ModelCounting, and every count hides a choicePut a famous PNAS paper on trial, build a word-counter by hand, watch the same sentence get counted three ways, and count an image too.
Week 3Classification: Counting with WeightsClassification. Train a reader and read what it learnedTeach a machine a bias in ten minutes, build a classifier and read its mind, and preview the full methods menu before next week's commitment.
Week 4Pick a Corpus. Pick Two Methods. Commit.The project (no new mechanism this week)Leave with a pitched project, two chosen methods, your Data Biography drafted, and the skill that makes "bring your own corpus" real: getting data off the web.
Week 5Embeddings: A Map of MeaningEmbeddings, the heart of the course, the leap past countingWatch your own corpus, text or images, sort itself by meaning, see the finding counting couldn't give you, and learn the same trick is how "For You" feeds place you.
Week 6Deepen the Project: Images, and Where the AI FailsApply the tools harder; image projects' deepest day; calibrate the AI on your dataPush your project past first findings, give image projects their deepest day, and find exactly where the AI's reading of your data goes wrong.
Week 7The AI as a Reader, at ScaleLLM-as-annotator, the most common operation in the fieldUse the AI to read your whole corpus at once, learn to trust the labels it's sure of and catch the ones it isn't, and reckon with whose judgment you're renting.
Week 8Models as Time Capsules + Settle the FindingWhat a trained model is: a compression of its corpusMeet models that live entirely inside one historical period, learn what a famous model has secretly memorized, kill your own finding to see what survives, and leave with a prose draft.
Week 9Build the PagePublishing, Quarto to GitHub Pages, static by defaultLeave today with a deployed draft of your web essay at a public URL.
Week 10The ShowcaseA public celebration; the two capstone assessmentsA 90-minute public event followed by a reception. Snacks. Music. Friends and family invited. Printed program with everyone's titles and URLs.

Culture as Data: a 10-week, project-based community course. $0 per student. Built to run on Google Colab's free tier.
