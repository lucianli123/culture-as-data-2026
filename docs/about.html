About

Who it's for

Curious adults, mixed coding backgrounds, most with little or none. Project-based, not a survey.

Cost

$0 by default. The free Gemini tier covers it; a $20/month chat subscription is optional and never required.

The AI as a tool, not an oracle

The AI writes code and helps you build, but it has known failure modes: false confidence, shrinking your question to fit the tool, insecure code, weak non-English. Each session has a stretch with it closed, so you leave able to reproduce your own work.

The through-lines

A few threads run through every week, and each session advances at least one:

  • Beyond counting. Each tool reaches past the last; the signature move is re-asking a counting question by meaning.
  • Every method and chart is a choice. We admire finished work and interrogate it, then turn the same eye on our own.
  • The readers you can and can't see. The transparent classifier (Week 3), the powerful annotator (Week 7), and the model you fine-tune and own.
  • Whose data, whose judgment. The Data Biography, the licensing line, and the scraped creative work the models learned from.
  • The AI as an instrument, not an oracle. Known failure modes, and a stretch each session with the assistant closed.
  • Images are co-equal culture. You count, classify, and embed pictures the same way as text.

The showcase

By Week 10 you'll have a URL you can send to your group chat. The page will load. There will be a chart. There will be an image. There will be an argument. Strangers will read it.

Culture as Data: a 10-week, project-based community course. $0 per student. Built to run on Google Colab's free tier.
