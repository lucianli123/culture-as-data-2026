Before your first session

  • Open the smoke test above and run it top to bottom: a green report means your runtime is healthy.
  • Each notebook's first cells mount your Google Drive (so work survives a Colab reset) and install the few pinned packages Colab doesn't ship.
  • Week 7 wants a free Gemini API key in Colab Secrets; without one it runs end-to-end on a recorded response.
  • Weeks 5 and 7 also come in GUIDED (fuller scaffolding) and SKELETON (more blanks) versions; choose the one that matches your experience.

Getting social-media and fiction data

Ready-made loaders for the post-API era, used from Week 4 on. Each runs offline on a built-in sample; live pulls are opt-in.

  • Reddit, three routes, Historical archives on Hugging Face, the official API via PRAW, and a fiction loader for r/nosleep and r/HFY. Open in Colab
  • Bluesky firehose, The most open social corpus: no key, hundreds of thousands of public posts an hour. Open in Colab
  • Mastodon, Per-instance public timelines; the server you choose is a corpus choice. Open in Colab
  • Project Gutenberg, Public-domain genre fiction by ID: full text, zero legal risk. Open in Colab
  • Polite scraper template, The Letterboxd pattern with the delay and checklist built in; Goodreads via the UCSD Book Graph instead. Open in Colab

Handouts and the publishing template

  • Common-errors cheat sheet: what to do when a cell fails, handed out in Week 1.
